# Open Empirical Homiletics

> An open invitation to build the first distributed research collaborative on sermon effectiveness.

## What This Is

A pre-worked research design for empirically answering the question **"what measurable properties of sermons predict hearer-centered outcomes across traditions?"** Modeled on the [Psychological Science Accelerator](https://psysciacc.org/), [ManyBabies](https://manybabies.org/), and [ManyPrimates](https://manyprimates.github.io/pilot/). Designed for pre-registered, multi-tradition, distributed execution.

Everything here is released under **CC BY 4.0**. You can fork, adapt, rename, and execute the design without permission. Attribution requested.

## Status

**Seeking adopter.** The author is an independent Reformed Presbyterian pastor without the time, credentials, or institutional position to lead this work. The design is published here so someone with the right combination of training, time, and network can carry it forward.

## Quick Tour of the Design

- **Research question**: what measurable sermon properties predict comprehension, encoding, activation across traditions
- **Four constructs** (not one): rhetorical craft, immediate reception efficacy, transformative activation, historical influence (secondary)
- **Two panels**: expert homileticians (craft), lay listeners (reception + activation)
- **Rating method**: Best-Worst Scaling over Likert (Kiritchenko & Mohammad 2017)
- **Sample**: contemporary English-language sermons to adult N. American congregations, 2005+, 18-50 min, stratified across 5+ traditions
- **Features**: automatic (NLP + prosody), human-coded (Big Idea, FCF, application specificity, etc.), panel-rated
- **Pre-registration**: OSF; 10-15 confirmatory claims max; Campbell-Fiske MTMM construct validity
- **Analysis**: multilevel regression, Holm for confirmatory / BH for exploratory, specification-curve robustness, locked hold-out + cross-corpus replication
- **First study**: 20 sermons × 400 BWS raters × 5 traditions, RR at PCI RR / PLOS ONE, 18-month timeline

## How to Adopt

1. **Fork this repository.** Update the README to name yourself as maintainer.
2. **Read the full design document.** The ten minutes you'll spend are the single best investment in deciding whether this is for you.
3. **Refine the first-study protocol.** Narrow to exactly one primary outcome. Tighten the coding manual for human-coded features.
4. **Open an issue here** letting the original author know you've adopted, so he can (a) redirect inquiries to you and (b) walk away cleanly.
5. **Run the Phase 0 checklist** in the full document. Key gate: methodologist co-lead secured and Societas or Academy umbrella confirmed by month 3.
