Colleagues,

I am writing to share a research design I believe the field would benefit from executing but which I cannot personally lead. I am releasing it under a Creative Commons license in hopes that someone with the right combination of time, training, and institutional position will take it up.

## The Question

What measurable properties of sermons actually correlate with hearer-centered outcomes (comprehension, encoding, activation) across traditions? The claims of Robinson, Chapell, Beeke, Augustine, Chrysostom, and classical rhetoric are asserted, taught, and debated but rarely empirically tested. Modern methodologies in computational rhetoric (Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil, Gennaro & Ash) and communication research (Green & Brock, Bullock et al., Kiritchenko & Mohammad) now make rigorous testing feasible. To my knowledge, no one has combined the two in an open, pre-registered, multi-tradition study.

## What's Ready

A full research design is published at **[SocArXiv DOI]** and on GitHub at **[repository URL]** under CC BY 4.0. It includes:

- Four-construct decomposition (rhetorical craft / reception efficacy / transformative activation / historical influence)
- Multi-DV measurement design with both expert and lay panels
- Feature operationalization rules (automatic, human-coded, panel-rated, with strict limits on LLM-derived features in confirmatory analyses)
- Pre-registration template (Campbell-Fiske MTMM; STROBE; AsPredicted hybrid)
- Multilevel statistical analysis plan with Holm / BH correction and specification-curve robustness checks
- Governance model borrowed from Psychological Science Accelerator and ManyBabies
- A concrete first study: 20 sermons × 5 traditions × 400 BWS lay raters × one primary outcome, targeted at PCI Registered Reports
- IRB path (Advarra or institutional co-PI), funding path (Louisville Institute bridge → Templeton → Lilly institutional)
- Named candidate collaborators across traditions
- Honest failure modes and a gated 12-month go/no-go structure

## Who I Am — and What I'm Not

I am a Reformed Presbyterian pastor preaching weekly, with no institutional affiliation and no research credentials. I developed the design through extensive consultation with frontier AI research agents and the published methodology literature. The design is credible. My capacity to execute it is not.
