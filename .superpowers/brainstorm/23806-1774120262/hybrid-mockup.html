ἀποκαλύπτεται — "is revealed" — this is present passive. Is God actively revealing His wrath right now, or is this eschatological?
Great catch on the present tense. Cranfield argues it's both — the present passive indicates an ongoing revelation through the natural order (vv. 19-20) AND through the "handing over" process in vv. 24, 26, 28. Hodge takes a similar view but emphasizes the judicial character — God is not passive here.
Hodge, Romans: "The wrath of God is not an emotion, but the judicial displeasure of God against sin, and his purpose to punish it."
How does this affect your sermon? If wrath is being revealed now, that changes the urgency for your congregation.
Yes — that's the FCF. They think wrath is future but Paul says look around you. The "giving over" IS the wrath.
